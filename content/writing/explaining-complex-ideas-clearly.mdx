Tutoring Discrete Mathematics and Numerical Methods taught me that understanding a concept is not the same as being able to explain it well.

### What changed for me

- I became more careful about sequence: people understand better when ideas are introduced in the right order.
- I learned that examples often matter more than perfect definitions in the early stages of learning.
- I started designing interfaces and writing documents with the same question in mind: what would reduce confusion fastest?

The biggest lesson is simple: clarity is a practical skill, not just a stylistic preference.
